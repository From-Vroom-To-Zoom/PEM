Reverse Engeneering

In this fase we dedicated ourselfs to exploring and understanding how the RC car works. We disassembled it and measured every part, so we could model it using a dedicated software.

This fase was crucial to know exactly the space we had to put the new components.

Research

In this fase we investigated and studied the diferent options of motors, batteries and speed controllers, so we could take the best aproach and the choose the components that would better fit the car.

Motor and ESC

After researching we found that for the motor, there are two types, brushed and brushless. Brushless motors are more eficient, less noisy, last longer and are more precise, for those reasons we decided that a brushless motor was more suting for what we needed.

When we chose the motor, we wanted to have an equal top speed to the combustion version of the RC car (around 70 km/h). Using the gear ratio of our car (1:11.72 given by the manual), the tire diametre and the top speed (70 km/h) we calculated the maximum RPM that we wanted our motor to be runnig at (around 40,000 RPM).

An important value that defines the motor is the "Kv", which is the ratio beetween the maximum RPM and the voltage of the battery. It was decided after the reaserch fase that the battery would have 14.8 V which meant that the ideal Kv for our motor would be 2700 [RPM/V]

For the ESC (eletronic speed controller) we decided to just choose one that the brand of the chosen motor recomended, to avoid any compatibility isues.

After searching for a combo for our 1/8 RC car, our final decision for the motor and ESC, came to be the "QuicRun 4268SL" for the motor and the "QuicRun ESC WP8BL150" for the ESC.

Battery

After the research made in the second fase, we found that for the battery we had two options, we could use LiPo or Ni-MH batteries. LiPo batteries have a bigger power density then Ni-MH batteries, which means that for the same voltage we could have have a smaller battery, ideal for the small space we had to put the new components.

To be compatible with the motor we needed a four cell battery equivalent to 14.8 V.

A variable we also had to look at was the C-Rating, which is the discharge rate of the battery, to discover the minimum C-Rating we need to look at the current of the ESC we previously chose (150 A) and for the current in mAh that our battery will have. Because we decided we wanted a battery around 5000 mAh, the minimum C-Rating equals 30C.

Acording to our research the C-Rating should be around 1.5 times the value of the minimum C-Rating, to assure safety and to avoid over heating, so we decided to look for a battery with a C-Rating of 45C.

After considering all the variables and the space we had in the car we chose the "Sigma 45C 5200 mAh" as our battery.

Controller

We decided to build a new controller and receiver, instead of trying to fix the ones we had, for the purpose of enabling more customization and also learning how the control of the car works on a more fundamental level.

After some experiments and research we found that communication using radio was the only good option we had, offering the best values in latency tests and being the only one to consistently provide the range we needed. In the field of radio communication there were two main modules we found that would suit our needs, the lora and the nrf24l01, we had used a lora module for our latency tests, however we found in our research that in theory the nrf24l01 can be a bit faster with the trade off of having a little less range, which we found to better overall for our project, at least in theory. Our tests found the NRF module harder to work with than the lora, so maybe do keep that in mind if you attempt a similar project.

For the processing of inputs and outputs we used two Esp32Wrooms, one on the car and one on the controller, the decision simply came down to what we had acess two, and would find easier to work with. Most microcontrollers could probably get the job done, with more or less complex circuits, this is simply the one we chose.

For the inputs on our controller we are gonna have two joysticks, one for accelaration and anotehr for direction, we will also implement a switch that toggles the car between a "fast mode" and a "slow mode", where the slow mode limits the max power of the motor, making it drive slower in order to be used, for example, in indoor spaces.
